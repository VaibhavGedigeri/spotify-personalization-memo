Core argument

Spotify must shift from a model-centric architecture — where the algorithm is the product — to a context-centric one, where understanding the full human behind the listening session is the product. No competitor has the cross-format behavioral depth to replicate this. But the window to build it is not permanent.

The architectural shift

Context-centric personalization: four layers

01  LAYER
Listener Context Graph
Persistent, cross-format identity model encoding long-term taste, medium-term behavioral patterns, and real-time situational signals — assembled dynamically at inference.
02  LAYER
Continuous Intent Model
Treats the full behavioral stream between sessions as a running inference problem — proactive, not reactive. Surfaces the right audio before the listener articulates what they want.
03  LAYER
Generative Retrieval
Generates the path to what a listener needs rather than selecting from a ranked list — including across format boundaries. Grounded in Semantic ID infrastructure already in production.
04  LAYER
Explainability as Product
Natural language context around every recommendation — proven to increase discovery willingness and generate richer feedback signals than behavioral data alone.

Internal platform imperatives

Three things the platform must become

EDD
Eval-Driven Development
Profile-aware LLM-as-judge evals, version-controlled alongside every context and prompt change. Gartner: 85% of GenAI projects fail without this.
CEP
Context Engineering Platform
Shared APIs for assembling, compressing, versioning and tracing listener context — so every product team draws from the same well, not their own rebuild.
LPM
Large Personalization Model
One unified generative architecture — grounded in Semantic IDs — that product teams fork, evaluate, and merge back via quality gates, not dozens of models drifting apart.
